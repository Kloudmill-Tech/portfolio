# Cookies in Plain English

----

## Overview of Cookies in Browsers

:memo: A **Cookie** is a text file embedded in web browers that serve a specific purpose. Automatically saved to our browsers, after navigating a website, cookiestrack user activity. Think of this text file as marker that notes our behavior and report back to the website owner. Additionally, there used by marketing companies to deliever ads tailored to your interest. Searching for snowboards, will deliver ads on planning your next ski trip to Aspen, Colorado. That is the magic or albeit technical marvel of cookies. Below are a few steps on navigating the discret world of cookies:

* Clearing Cookies and Why?
The term 'clear your cookies' actually means removing the saved cookie file from your browser. Clearing your cookies, offers the following benefits:

1. Reduction of targeted Ads
2. Improved browser performance
3. Old data is flushed

* What does a Cookie look Like?
Here is an example of a JSON style cookie file, using a browser format

```JSON
'"domain": ".example.com", "hostOnly": false, "path": "/", "secure": true, "httpOnly": true, "sameSite": "Strict", "expirationDate": 1893456000, "name": "auth_token", "value": "A1B2C3D4E5F6G7H8" },'
```

* Why am getting so many pop-ups?

----

This age old question is due to too many cookies saved in your browser. Marketing firms use cookies to target specific audiences with precise ads or pop-ups. As a result your web browsing is slow, inefficent and virus prone.

## Conclusion

Cookies, are widely used in modern websites and ultimately end users like yourself must exercise their right to remove them.
